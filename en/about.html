Dongfang Tenghong

Founder of Eastern Mystical Imagery Painting

Dongfang Tenghong

Dongfang Tenghong (formerly Zhang Jun, also known as Dongfang Yiren), born in 1962 in Chengdu, Sichuan, with ancestral roots in Linhai, Zhejiang. He is the founder of "Eastern Mystical Imagery Painting" in China's contemporary art world. Over four decades of creation, his work evolved from early realism into profound philosophical expressions of life totems, establishing a unique Eastern aesthetic narrative system.

Artistic Philosophy

"Recast serenity in the midst of clamor, perceive eternity in the void"

Eastern Mystical Imagery

Dongfang Tenghong's revolutionary approach employs hyperrealistic technique to depict the absolute void—a paradoxical marriage of extreme technical precision and metaphysical emptiness. This contradiction becomes the gateway to transcendence, where the visible world dissolves into infinite possibility.

The "magical light" that emanates from within his paintings originates not from external sources, but from the inner spiritual realm. It is a luminescence born from meditation and consciousness, illuminating the eternal within the temporary, the infinite within the finite.

Three archetypal symbols anchor his visual language: the white horse, representing the soul's projection and boundless freedom; the red monk, embodying spiritual discipline and the anchor of consciousness; and the highland plateau, depicting a purified world beyond mundane reality. Together, they form a cosmology of Eastern transcendence.

Oil painting "Journey" selected for the "55th Anniversary of the Army Founding National Art Exhibition" (China Military Museum)

1983

Oil painting "Sister" participated in "Sichuan Native Painting Exhibition" (Sichuan Fine Arts Museum)

1984

Oil painting "Beyond the River" selected for "Sichuan Art Exhibition" (Sichuan Fine Arts Museum). Inspired by Zhu Ziqing's essay "Back View," the work profoundly depicted the silhouette of a Sichuan farmer and drew widespread response in the art world

1985

Oil painting "Window" selected for "Advancing Chinese Youth National Art Exhibition" and won Bronze Award, one of the highest awards among participants from the military and Sichuan. This exhibition is an important milestone in Chinese art history, igniting the '85 Art Movement (China Art Museum)

1986

Four oil paintings "Small White Flower," "Silent Night," "New Moon," and "Army Soul" simultaneously selected for "National Military Art Exhibition," with the most entries selected nationwide. "Small White Flower" won the highest award—Excellence Prize (China Art Museum)

1987

Oil painting "Outside the Window" selected for "60th Anniversary of the Army Founding National Art Exhibition" and won Excellence Award (China Art Museum)
